1. Every time `/career-ops oferta` generates Block F (Interview Plan), new STAR+R stories get appended here

3. The "Big Three" questions can be answered with stories from this bank:

### [Systems / Process Improvement] Delivering a Planning System Implementation Under an Inherited Mandate
**Source:** Jeremy (Unique Appliances) — added 2026-05-03, corrected 2026-05-19
**Company context:** Special Projects Analyst promoted to Director, Supply Chain & Technology — 500+ SKUs, 3-4 distribution centers, manual spreadsheet-driven planning environment

**S (Situation):** Unique Appliances had signed a 3-year Slim4 demand planning contract and scoped the implementation before Jeremy joined. Both the VP and SC Manager involved in the original scoping had since departed, leaving the project without an owner. The existing planning environment was entirely manual and spreadsheet-driven - cell-by-cell number entry with no ERP connection, no exception-based workflows, and limited visibility into demand, inventory, or service risk across the SKU base.

**T (Task):** Pick up an orphaned implementation mandate as sole functional owner and deliver the Slim4 + NetSuite integration to specification - translating the contracted scope into working planning logic without the institutional knowledge of the people who scoped it.

**A (Action):** Served as lead business analyst and functional architect for the full delivery - mapped 10-15 core data domains (items, locations, vendors, lead times, MOQs, lifecycle status, in-transit, service levels) into Slim4, defined 30-50 planning parameters including safety stock logic, service level targets, and replenishment triggers, and acted as the primary translator between business users, NetSuite data structures, and Slim4's planning engine. Delivered across a 6-month timeline (Apr-Oct 2024) working with supply chain, IT, and external consultants. Post-launch, drove further enhancements - added a new Savannah DC with full data and decision mapping, and implemented an allocation module enabling flexible PO destination management and mid-stream PO revisions to adapt to a rapidly shifting operating environment.

**R (Result):** Implementation delivered to specification on time. The integration replaced manual cell-by-cell spreadsheet entry with a direct NetSuite-connected planning environment - designed to reduce planning prep time by ~80% (from ~10 hours to ~2 hours) through automated data sync and exception-based workflows. Recognized by leadership with performance compensation and promoted to Director, Supply Chain & Technology effective November 1, 2024.

**Reflection:** Picking up an orphaned project means you own the outcome but not the decisions that got you there. The discipline is to understand the original intent well enough to deliver it faithfully - then make the right calls on the gaps the original team didn't anticipate. Post-launch, the bigger lesson was that no planning tool survives a fundamental change to the demand history it was built on - when tariffs rewrote the product mix and supply base simultaneously, the algorithm's forecasting foundation was gone. The right pivot was to S&OP infrastructure, not more tool features.

**Best for questions about:**

**T (Task):** As sole procurement lead, manage total delivered cost optimization across a complex, evolving multi-vendor, multi-country supply base - and ensure the business was positioned to execute an immediate pivot when external conditions demanded it.

**A (Action):** Applied total delivered cost as the consistent lens across every vendor, routing, and inventory decision - balancing unit cost, freight, tariff exposure, lead times, and supply continuity simultaneously on every PO cycle. Built procurement execution readiness alongside the product development team's diversification work. ### [Process Improvement / Six Sigma] $430K Freight Payment Savings via DMAIC — Project Confluence
**Source:** Jeremy (LifeScan / J&J) — added 2026-05-14
**Company context:** Supply Chain & Operations Leader — LifeScan Canada, ~$32M in annual freight payments processed through CT Logistics across 46K bills; J&J Process Excellence program

**S (Situation):** LifeScan's freight payment process with CT Logistics had a 15% match fail rate - invoiced freight amounts frequently mismatched expected payments, requiring manual web resolution (webres). EDI adoption was stuck at 60%, on-time resolution was 74%, and average resolution cycle time was 6.5 days. Across 46K bills and ~$32M in processed freight, this was generating significant cost leakage and operational drag.

**T (Task):** Lead a structured DMAIC improvement project (Project Confluence) to reduce freight payment mismatches, accelerate resolution, and drive EDI adoption - with the goal of qualifying for J&J Process Excellence Lean Green Belt certification.

**A (Action):** Applied full Six Sigma DMAIC methodology across all five phases:
- **Define:** Established CTQs (match fail rate, resolution cycle time, EDI adoption), mapped the high-level freight pay process, identified champion/process owner/team, built project plan and milestones with CT Logistics as the key partner
- **Measure:** Defined defect, unit, and opportunity metrics; built a detailed process map of the webres workflow; validated the measurement system; collected baseline data and established sigma baseline across 46K bills
- **Analyze:** Identified value vs. non-value added steps; isolated root causes of mismatch (EDI gaps, carrier exceptions, UPS SCS outlier behavior); determined vital few x's driving both fail rate and cycle time
- **Improve:** Developed and piloted solutions targeting EDI adoption and exception-handling workflows; validated improvement through pilot studies; corrected for UPS SCS outlier in metric reporting
- **Control:** Implemented monitoring and control systems; developed standards and procedures; determined final process capability; transferred to process owner with full documentation

**R (Result):** $430K in net savings (net of $29K in CT Logistics fees). Match fail rate trended from 15% down to ~2% by December (adjusted for UPS SCS outlier, which had an ongoing systemic issue outside project scope) — the 2% target was the project's projected end-state, confirmed by December actuals trending to that level. EDI adoption increased from 60% to the high 80s. On-time resolution improved from 74% to 80%. Resolution cycle time cut from 6.5 to 5 days. Earned J&J Process Excellence Lean Green Belt certification.

**Note on the 2% figure:** This is the adjusted match fail rate (UPS SCS removed). The unadjusted rate was higher due to an ongoing systemic UPS SCS issue outside the project's control. Always use "approximately 2%" — a cover letter drafted in 2026 incorrectly stated "0.02%" which was a typo.

**Reflection:** The UPS SCS outlier was the most important lesson - not all variation is yours to solve. Recognizing which root causes are within your control vs. outside it lets you set honest targets and avoid claiming credit (or blame) for factors you can't move.

**Best for questions about:**
- "Do you have Lean or Six Sigma experience?"
- "Tell me about a DMAIC project you worked on"
- "Tell me about a time you led a process improvement initiative"
- "How do you use data to identify and eliminate waste?"
- "Tell me about a time you drove cost savings through process change"
